Hematocrit Case Study: Severe Polycythemia

Patient Profile

Name: Robert H.

Age: 62

Sex: Male

Chief Complaint: Increasing fatigue, headaches, and shortness of breath.

Robert is a 62-year-old male with a long history of chronic lung disease related to smoking. He reports worsening fatigue over the past 6 months, frequent headaches, and occasional dizziness. He also notes his skin appears slightly reddish, especially on his face.

Medical History: Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), 40-year smoking history.

Vital Signs:
Heart Rate: 92 bpm
Respiratory Rate: 22 breaths/min
Blood Pressure: 148/90 mmHg
Oxygen Saturation: 88%

Physical Exam: Ruddy complexion, mild clubbing of fingers, labored breathing.

Clinical Note: Chronic low oxygen levels can stimulate the body to produce more red blood cells, increasing hematocrit.
